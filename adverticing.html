Posters for exhibition "Constructivism in graphic design"

 

Russian Constructivism characteristically had minimal color palettes, often just red, black and sometimes yellow. They frequently had diagonal elements with circular and angled type and images. The resulting work was extremely dramatic, containing layered images coupled with powerful type treatments.

Vladimir Stenberg and Georgii Stenberg were Soviet artists and designers.

The Stenberg's primary technique was montage. Their posters were designed to be eye-catching and even shocking. They are frequently used for unconventional viewing angles, radical foreshortening, unsettling close-up and disregarding actual proportions ... turning figures upside down. Their posters even hold up today, and can appear as striking as they did in their day.

Requirements:

The style of posters should display actual topic and style.
